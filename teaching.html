Brian C Jenkins | Teaching

Summary

I started teaching my own economics courses in the summer of 2009 as a graduate student at the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill (UNC). Since then, I have taught 30 economics courses at four universities: UNC, the University of Montana, the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), and the University of California, Irvine (UCI). I've taught students at all levels: principles, intermediate, and graduate. I have experience teaching classes with enrollments as small as 10 students to large lecture classes with up to 450 students. Student evaluations of my teaching are consistently excellent and continue to improve. My colleagues in the Economics Department at the University of Montana rated my teaching for the 2011-12 academic year as "outstanding".

In addition to classroom experience, I have been involved in a number of other activities that complement my teaching. I currently serve as the director of the Economics Learning Center: a new tutoring initiative for UCI students enrolled in principles and intermediate-level economics classes. At the University of Montana I supervised 5 senior thesis research projects. As a graduate student at UNC, I completed the Teacher Training Program (TTP) provided by the Department of Economics. Finally, teaching large lecture sections at UCI has given me valuable experience working with and delegating responsibilities to graduate teaching assistants.
